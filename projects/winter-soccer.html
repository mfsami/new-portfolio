Winter Soccer League Site.

Winter 2023 - Present

Stack: React, HTML, CSS

A local winter soccer tournament needed a simple way to track player stats, standings, teams, and weekly highlights across a yearly three-week season. I was referred by my friend Adnan, who told me that they needed someone who could design it cleanly and ship it quickly. So I took it on and built the leagues site.

“We need a site where players can see standings, top scorers, highlights and anything related to the tournament”

Immediatley got to work building up wireframes and designs in Figma then transferring it over to clean code. This became the first full React project I completed end-to-end. The very first version of this site in 2023 was a plain HTML/CSS site, I sort of cringe looking back at it. The next year I redesigned it in ReactJS with a cleaner layout, better typography, and a mobile-first flow, as most players would access it off of their phones. This was my first time having to deal with clients. Every new idea or devleopment I had was always ran through the tournament owners before committing. This established a pretty good developer client relationship. The site now pulls together teams, fixtures, results, leaderboards, and media in one place.

I’m the dedicated developer and maintainer each winter, keeping everything updated during the tournament. The crew liked the site so much they even drafted me onto a team... so yes, I now track my own stats too. This project taught me a ton about practical web dev: structuring data, state and UI, and designing for speed when people are checking scores on the sideline. I’ve got a backlog of enhancements I plan to add next season.
